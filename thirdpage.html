    During the 1929-1930 season, a green clover leaf was used by the club as their emblem for forty years until the club adopted a crest. It was a three-leaf clover, which typically symbolizes love, hope, and faith. Considering that the club previously used a Celtic cross, the clover leaf could also stand for the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit.
